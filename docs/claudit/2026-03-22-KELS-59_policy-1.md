### ~~1. Edge policy constraint checks exact SAID match, not canonical (compacted) match~~ — RESOLVED

**File:** `lib/kels-creds/src/verification.rs:252-261`

~~The edge verification checks `*expected_policy != edge_credential.policy` — a direct SAID comparison. Per the design (docs/design/policy.md and the plan), edge policy constraints should use **canonical policy matching**: compact the presented credential's policy and compare the canonical SAID to the edge's policy SAID. **Resolution:** Edge verification now resolves the edge credential's policy, compacts it to canonical form via `Policy::compact()`, and compares the canonical SAID to `edge.policy`. This enables delegate flexibility — the edge doesn't need updating when delegates rotate.

## Positive Observations

- **Clean DSL design.** The four AST node types (`Endorse`, `Delegate`, `Weighted`, `Policy`) compose naturally. `threshold()` in the DSL is syntactic sugar for `Weighted` with unit weights, keeping the AST minimal while the user-facing syntax stays expressive. The recursive descent parser is well-structured with position-aware error messages. The canonical round-trip (parse → display → parse) invariant is tested explicitly.

- **Policy compaction is elegant.** Stripping delegates from `delegate(DELEGATOR, DELEGATE)` to produce `delegate(DELEGATOR)` for canonical matching enables fleet scaling without edge updates — a genuinely novel capability with no KERI equivalent. The implementation is clean: `compact()` on the AST, recompute SAID.

- **Poison expression evaluated as a full DSL.** Rather than a simple list of authorized poisoners, the poison field is a full policy expression supporting thresholds (e.g., "2-of-3 admins must agree to poison"). The evaluation is well-separated — main expression evaluates with immune effective policy, poison expression evaluates separately using the poison hash as the anchor.

- **Endorsement caching prevents redundant KEL verification.** The `evaluate_endorser` function caches results in the `endorsements` BTreeMap by prefix, so the same endorser appearing in multiple branches of a weighted tree only triggers one KEL verification. This is important for performance in complex policies.

- **Mutual exclusion of `immune` and `poison` is enforced at construction.** `Policy::build()` rejects `immune: true` with a poison expression, preventing contradictory policies. The two-field model (`poison: Option<String>`, `immune: Option<bool>`) cleanly represents three distinct modes.

- **Issuance/anchoring separation.** `Credential::build()` constructs and validates the credential, returning the canonical SAID. The caller anchors the SAID in endorser KELs separately. This clean separation makes multi-party endorsement natural — each endorser calls `interact()` independently rather than coupling construction to a single builder.
