## Positive Observations

- **Synchronous archival is provably bounded.** The proactive ror invariant (`MAX_NON_REVEALING_EVENTS = 62`) guarantees the adversary chain never exceeds one page. Combined with synchronous in-merge archival, recovery completes atomically in a single transaction — no background tasks, no TOCTOU, no partial states.

- **`send_divergent_events` correctly handles all 4 divergence geometries.** Recovered (owner longer/shorter), unrecovered, and contested KELs are each mapped to the correct submission sequence for the remote merge engine. The chain-building logic for contested KELs (tracing forward from fork events) is sound.

- **Defense-in-depth validation in `find_adversary_event`.** The serial-count invariant check (exactly 2 events at divergence serial, exactly 1 at all others) rejects tampered databases rather than silently proceeding with corrupt state. This is a good example of fail-secure design.

- **End-to-end contest regression tests.** `test_contest_on_divergent_kel_with_cnt_serial_above_diverged_at` and `test_contest_creates_divergence_on_linear_kel` are directly targeted at the round 3 bug fix, providing strong regression protection against the specific `cnt_serial` vs `diverged_at` issue.

- **Comprehensive reconciliation test suite.** The 10-scenario test-reconciliation.sh covers the full reconciliation proof matrix: recovered KEL propagation, post-recovery events, adversary rotation chains, contested KEL propagation (both chain orderings), contest during active archival, double recovery rejection, effective SAID convergence, and terminal state rejection. Helper functions for convergence waiting and archived event assertions are well-designed.

- **Clean `RecoveryRecord` design.** Single immutable audit record per recovery, written atomically in the merge transaction. The `owner_first_serial` field enables reconstruction of what was archived. No state machine, no versioning — just a terminal audit trail.
